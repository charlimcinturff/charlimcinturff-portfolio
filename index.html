About

I’m naturally curious about how complex systems work and how they can be improved, redesigned, or fixed when they break. Most of my interests sit between engineering, data, and design. I’ve worked on electrical design projects, spent a lot of time modeling and programming, and tutor calculus on campus.

Teaching math has helped me explain complicated ideas clearly and break difficult problems into smaller, manageable parts. I approach challenges through systems thinking: understanding inputs, constraints, feedback loops, and how small design changes can significantly improve performance.

Lately, I’ve become especially interested in simulation and modeling. I enjoy using computational tools to explore how systems behave before they are built or implemented, whether that means simulating operational processes, testing dynamic behavior, or analyzing how parameter changes affect outcomes.

Outside of engineering, I’m an avid painter and a committed music listener. Both keep me creative and remind me that strong design often lives somewhere between structure, experimentation, and imagination.

Bayesian Swarm Search Model (MATLAB)

Featured

Bayes’ Theorem • Swarm Intelligence • Agent-Based Modeling

Bayesian ant simulation
SEARCHING
PAIRS 0
PILE 1
Model: live ants + corpse piling + Bayesian searcher
Behavior: smooth steering, avoidance, pile formation

In early February I had attented a lecture on Swarm Intelligence which was a new concept to me. This talk had peaked my curiosity and become a concept I wanted to work more with. This simulation models swarm search using Bayes’ Theorem as the decision engine. Each agent updates a belief state from observations and interactions, allowing search behavior to emerge from repeated probabilistic updates.

  • Search actions driven by posterior probability updates
  • Decentralized behavior with no global controller
  • Stochastic motion constrained to a bounded environment
  • Clear visualization with robust contact handling
